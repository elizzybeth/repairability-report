Problem: ASUS and others are manipulating the FRI to give themselves 10/10 repairability on devices that are anything but.

Narrative: What they did right, what they did wrong, and why we’re pissed off that they gave themselves a 10/10.

We’re picking on ASUS but this applies to a whole bunch of manufacturers (name and shame a couple more in conclusion).

Service Manual

Customer Self Repair (CSR)

iFixit score (tablet): 7.4/10

FRI score (laptop): 10/10

Notes to incorporate into script

  • Serial number needed for easy access to drivers and manuals. Otherwise it’s a bit buried.[a]
  • CSR wording is unfavourable to consumers. Link to FAQ detailing how to order SSD replacement is dead.

  • Scored as a laptop or tablet? Pros and cons, loopholes, what it means for the consumer, etc
  • Build a case for why we’re scoring it as a tablet. Confirm it’s not a 10/10 on either scorecard (soldered RAM on a laptop).
  • The service manual is incomplete but good.
  • Parts availability is scarce and extremely difficult to locate (across multiple sites, no clear endorsement from ASUS) unless you have a working laptop and are able to auto diagnose faults with the ASUS diagnostic tool. Some repairs would prevent this from being a viable option including RAM and SSD failure. Other failures like wireless card, screen, keyboard, trackpad, would require using external devices (wireless dongle, monitor, etc) which consumers may not have access to.

Disassembly Notes

  • Screen comes away first. Didn’t notice any glue, or if there was any it was extremely light. Chances of removing the screen without damage are excellent.
  • Battery can be disconnected as soon as the screen comes off. Removal requires disconnecting two more flex cables. Screws, no glue.
  • M.2 is accessible without disassembly.
  • The
  • One of two fans is trapped underneath the heatsink.
  • Rear camera module is immediately accessible after screen removal.
  • Front facing camera module is immediately accessible after screen removal.
  • Speakers are buried under battery but easy to remove.
  • MicroSD reader is modular, but buried under the battery, speaker, and one of the kickstand hinges.
  • I/O board is modular but buried under the battery, speaker, and the other kickstand hinge.
  • The thermals comprise two sets of components: heatsink and two fans. The fans are separate components but only one of the two fans can be removed without removing the heatsink. The SoC employs liquid metal, making removal and replacement of the heatsink riskier than necessary if the goal is to clean the fans.
  • Removing the motherboard was relatively simple once the case was clear of components. Two coax cables need to be removed along with a few screws.
  • Both the RAM modules and the wireless module are soldered to the motherboard.
  • All the ports are soldered to the motherboard.
  • Several other minor components can be removed (kickstand, wireless antennas, pogo pin connectors, etc) but we did not remove these. Overall, all these components are highly modular and replaceable.
  • Very few markings on the silkscreen and device chassis. M.2 drive marked on the chassis.
